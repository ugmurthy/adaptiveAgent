IPL 2026: A Season of Records, Drama & Stellar Performances

Cameron Green breaks auction records as KKR make biggest splash; CSK bet big on youth talent

Auction Day: When Records Were Made

The TATA IPL 2026 Mini Auction held on December 16, 2025, at the Etihad Arena in Abu Dhabi witnessed history being made as franchises collectively spent ₹237.55 crore to acquire 77 players from a pool of 369 shortlisted cricketers.

The auction saw Australian all-rounder Cameron Green become the most expensive overseas player in IPL history when Kolkata Knight Riders secured his services for a staggering ₹25.20 crore. The bidding war involved Mumbai Indians, Rajasthan Royals, Chennai Super Kings, and ultimately KKR emerged victorious.

"This was not just an auction; it was a statement of intent by all franchises. The investment in young Indian talent alongside established international stars shows the league's evolution."

Team-by-Team Analysis: Who Won the Auction?

🟡 Chennai Super Kings - The Youth Revolution

CSK emerged as the biggest spenders at approximately ₹52.40 crore, making two record-breaking purchases of uncapped Indian players Prashant Veer and Kartik Sharma at ₹14.20 crore each. Their strategy clearly signals a bold investment in India's next generation of fast-bowling talent.

Key acquisitions include Rahul Chahar (₹5.20 Cr), Akeal Hosein (₹2 Cr), Matt Henry (₹2 Cr), and Sarfaraz Khan (₹75 L).

🟣 Kolkata Knight Riders - Star Power Unleashed

Entering the auction with the largest purse of ₹64.30 crore, KKR wasted no time in making their presence felt. They landed both Cameron Green and Matheesha Pathirana, spending approximately ₹62.90 crore overall. Their squad balance is now formidable with additions like Finn Allen, Rachin Ravindra, and Mustafizur Rahman.

🔴 Royal Challengers Bengaluru - Defending Champions Strike Smart

As defending champions, RCB approached the auction with precision. They focused on filling specific gaps, bringing in Venkatesh Iyer (₹7 Cr) as a powerful left-handed option and Suyash Sharma (₹4.50 Cr) as wrist-spin reinforcement. Total spend: ₹13.50 crore.

🟠 Sunrisers Hyderabad - Targeted Acquisition

SRH made one major statement buy — securing England's Liam Livingstone for ₹13 crore. His ability to bat explosively in the middle order and contribute with off-spin fills a crucial gap in SRH's lineup. Total spend: ₹18.50 crore.

🩷 Rajasthan Royals - Rebuilding After Trades

After significant trades involving Sanju Samson, Ravindra Jadeja, and Sam Curran, RR had substantial rebuilding to do. They picked up Ravi Bishnoi (₹7.20 Cr) and Prithvi Shaw (₹3.20 Cr). Total spend: ₹22.00 crore.
