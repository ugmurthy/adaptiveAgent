CRITICALRussia-Ukraine: Diplomatic Breakthroughs Derail

The Russia-Ukraine conflict has entered its fifth year with no end in sight, despite what appeared to be historic breakthroughs earlier this year. In February 2026, trilateral negotiations between Ukraine, Russia, and the United States achieved significant progress, only to be derailed by the ongoing Iran crisis that consumed American foreign policy attention.

Current Status as of June 2026
Conflict Duration: Entering Year 5
Latest Development: Competing ceasefire proposals remain stalled
Diplomatic Status: Direct negotiations not resumed

Competing Ceasefire Proposals

Both Moscow and Kyiv have announced competing ceasefire proposals, but these remain deadlocked. In May 2026, Russia announced a limited ceasefire for Victory Day commemorations (May 8-9), while Ukraine proposed an open-ended ceasefire. However, Russia rejected President Zelenskyy's peace proposal, refusing to give up any territory it has taken – approximately one-fifth of Ukraine.

Direct negotiations between Kyiv and Moscow have not resumed, and diplomatic gestures continue to be overshadowed by battlefield violence. In May 2026 alone, deadly Russian strikes killed at least 27 people in eastern Ukraine, underscoring the gap between diplomatic rhetoric and military reality.

Key Challenge

The fundamental obstacle remains territorial control: Russia's refusal to withdraw from occupied Ukrainian territory has created an impasse that neither side appears willing to compromise on.

STALLEDIsrael-Palestine: Neither War Nor Peace

Six months into a ceasefire that began on October 10, 2025, the situation in Gaza remains described by observers as "neither war nor peace." While the US-mediated ceasefire agreement involved an international Board of Peace and included provisions for hostage exchanges and humanitarian aid, implementation has been fraught with challenges.

Current Status as of June 2026
Ceasefire Duration: Since October 10, 2025 (6+ months)
Casualties During Ceasefire: At least 700 killed
Negotiation Status: Stalled since January 2026

Trump's Peace Plan Stalls

President Trump's 20-point peace plan saw initial success with a pause in fighting, hostage exchange, and surge in humanitarian aid. However, the second phase of the plan, announced by US Special Envoy Steve Witkoff on January 14, 2026, has largely stalled. As of May 2026, negotiations remained deadlocked due to repeated Israeli attacks since the October ceasefire and fundamental disagreements over Hamas disarmament.

"Daily volatility and instability across all aspects of life characterize the current situation in Gaza."

The ceasefire period has witnessed at least 700 deaths, with ongoing violations by Israeli forces undermining confidence in the agreement's sustainability. Both sides appear unwilling to make the difficult compromises necessary for a lasting resolution.

CRITICALSudan: A Humanitarian Catastrophe Persists

Sudan's civil war reached the grim milestone of 1,000 days of fighting in early 2026, creating one of the world's most severe humanitarian crises. Despite numerous diplomatic initiatives, the conflict between the Sudanese army and the Rapid Support Forces (RSF) continues to devastate civilian populations.

Current Status as of June 2026
Conflict Duration: Over 1,000 days
Latest Initiative: Quad-led peace plan (US, Saudi Arabia, Egypt, UAE)
External Backing: Continued support from regional powers complicates resolution

The Quad Initiative

In February 2026, US President Donald Trump's advisor on Arab and African Affairs, Massad Boulos, announced that Washington and three Middle Eastern states – Saudi Arabia, Egypt, and the United Arab Emirates (collectively known as the Quad) – were close to finalizing a detailed initiative aimed at ending Sudan's war. The plan resembled the roadmap shared by the Quad in September 2025.

However, prospects for the Quad-led initiative remain limited in the short term. External backing of warring parties continues, with the United Arab Emirates and Saudi Arabia entrenched in their respective positions. Regional security dynamics are becoming increasingly complex, making sustainable peace elusive.

Peace Talks Resume in Cairo

Peace efforts resumed in Cairo with Egypt and the United Nations urging warring parties to agree to a nationwide humanitarian truce. While such arrangements can reduce violence and allow humanitarian access, they leave core political disputes unresolved. The latest effort shows potential for a temporary humanitarian pause rather than a lasting political settlement.

Expert Assessment

"Three possible scenarios have emerged for the war-torn country in 2026, but current realities suggest that a ceasefire and formal transition to civilian governance is the least likely."

CRITICALEthiopia-Eritrea: Tensions on the Brink

Relations between Eritrea and Ethiopia have deteriorated sharply, with both countries publicly signaling possible preparation for war. Longstanding territorial disputes, mistrust borne of multiple past conflicts, and renewed military mobilization along the Tigray border have sparked fears of another large-scale confrontation in the Horn of Africa.

Current Status as of June 2026
Tension Level: Critical – preparations for possible war
Core Issue: Ethiopia's demand for Red Sea access vs. Eritrea's opposition
Regional Risk: Potential merger with Sudan conflict

Tigray Peace Deal Unimplemented

A peace deal signed in Pretoria, South Africa, ended the fighting in Tigray, but failure to implement its terms – including fixing the status of Tigray within the federal republic – remains a factor in ongoing tensions. The TPLF was banned as a political party in 2025, following the war in Tigray and the subsequent peace deal.

Red Sea Access Dispute

Ethiopia's insistence on securing direct maritime access to the Red Sea has become a major flashpoint. Ethiopia has accused Eritrea of supporting insurgents in its internal conflicts, while Eritrea denies these claims and views Addis Ababa's demands as a provocation. These tensions threaten to pull neighboring Sudan into a broader regional conflict.

"The prospect of renewed war in northern Ethiopia between government forces, Tigray factions, and Eritrea risks pulling in neighbouring Sudan and merging two of the deadliest conflicts in recent history."

Election Complications

Ethiopia's 2026 parliamentary and regional elections – considered among the most significant political milestones in the country's modern history – have been clouded by crises in Amhara and Tigray regions. Conflicts have disrupted voting, and opposition groups have questioned the process integrity.

STALLEDMyanmar: Civil War Deepens

Five years after the February 2021 military coup, Myanmar continues to grapple with brutal civil war alongside the aftermath of a catastrophic earthquake that struck in early 2025. The conflict has resulted in massive displacement and ongoing human rights violations.

Current Status as of June 2026
Conflict Duration: 5+ years since 2021 coup
Key Players: Tatmadaw military vs. resistance forces and ethnic armed organizations
International Response: ASEAN pushing peace plans

ASEAN Peace Efforts

Southeast Asia's top diplomats have met to push a peace plan for Myanmar's civil war and finalize a nonaggression pact with China. However, these efforts struggle to maintain pace with the spreading violence on the ground. Domestic and international actors seeking change toward federal democracy must now think strategically about three trends in 2026: the post-election landscape, prospects for conflict de-escalation or ceasefires, and how to engage with China's prominent role in Myanmar politics.

Warning Signs

Myanmar faces being "on the path to self-destruction if violence does not end," according to UN assessments. The combination of ongoing civil war and natural disaster aftermath has created compounding humanitarian challenges.

FRAGILE PROGRESSYemen: Fragile Truce Holds

Since 2014, Yemeni government forces backed by a Saudi-led coalition and Houthi rebels have fought for full control of the fractured country. The internationally recognized authorities are largely based in the southern city of Aden, while the Houthis control the capital Sana'a and large areas of the north and west.

Current Status as of June 2026
Truce Status: Fragile but enduring
Frontlines: Largely frozen
Power Dynamics: Changing balance among anti-Houthi forces

Saudi Dialogue Efforts

Saudi Arabia pursues dialogue among all players in southern Yemen and seeks to extend the truce and reach a peace agreement with the Houthis. The kingdom aims to prevent Yemen from becoming a platform for attacks on Saudi security and economic interests. Negotiations between the Houthis and Saudi Arabia have taken place periodically, indicating openness from Riyadh to close the chapter of its involvement in Yemen's war.

US-Houthi Ceasefire Deal

Saudi Arabia welcomed a statement from the Sultanate of Oman announcing a ceasefire agreement in Yemen aimed at safeguarding international interests. However, the Houthis continue to restrict aid groups, and the frontlines remain frozen while the balance of power among anti-Houthi forces shifts.

"A 'fragile' situation persists as the UN envoy urges renewed political push amid rising tensions."

Current Status as of June 2026

Conclusion: The Diplomacy Gap

The global picture of peace efforts in mid-2026 reveals a troubling pattern: diplomacy is struggling to keep pace with violence. According to the International Crisis Group's analysis, several concerning trends have emerged:

  • Short-term solutions dominate: Temporary ceasefires and framework deals have become the norm rather than lasting peace agreements
  • Core disputes remain unresolved: While humanitarian pauses may reduce immediate violence, fundamental political questions go unanswered
  • External interference complicates resolution: Continued backing of warring parties by external powers undermines local ownership of peace processes
  • Regional crises divert attention: Emerging conflicts in Iran and Venezuela consume diplomatic bandwidth needed elsewhere
  • Human costs mount: Unless diplomacy and long-term peace planning are deliberately chosen by political leaders, the human cost of war will continue to rise
Looking Ahead

The gap between diplomatic gestures and battlefield reality remains the defining challenge of 2026. Political leaders worldwide face a choice: commit to sustained, principled diplomacy that addresses root causes of conflict, or accept a future where temporary ceasefires punctuate endless cycles of violence.

"The human cost of war continues to rise unless diplomacy and long-term peace planning are deliberately chosen by political leaders."
— International Crisis Group, L'Osservatorio Global Conflict Outlook 2026
